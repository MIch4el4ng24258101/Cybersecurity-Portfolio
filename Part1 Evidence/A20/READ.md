1. Biggest Cyber Threat Groups or Organizations
During the discussion, we identified three main types of cyber threat actors:

a. Nation-State Actors
Countries such as Russia, China, and North Korea were considered the most dangerous.

Why they are a major threat:
Highly funded and advanced capabilities
Conduct cyber espionage and cyber warfare
Target critical infrastructure (power grids, hospitals, government systems)
Example: State-sponsored attacks on energy systems and elections.

b. Cybercriminal Organizations
Groups like ransomware gangs operate for financial gain.

Examples include:
LockBit
ALPHV (BlackCat)
Threats they pose:
Ransomware attacks
Data breaches and extortion
Target businesses, hospitals, and individuals

2. Is Cyberspace Being Militarized?
Yes — cyberspace is increasingly becoming a domain of warfare, similar to land, sea, air, and space.

Key points discussed:
Governments are developing cyber armies
Cyberattacks are used in conflicts without physical violence
Attacks can disrupt entire nations (power, banking, communication)

Example: Cyber operations linked to conflicts like the Russia-Ukraine cyberwarfare

Conclusion:
Cyberspace is actively being militarized, and cyber warfare is now a core part of modern defense strategies.

3. Emerging Cyber Threats
We identified several growing threats:

a. AI-Powered Cyberattacks
Attackers use AI to automate hacking
Advanced phishing and deepfake scams

b. Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)
Cybercrime tools are sold online
Anyone can launch attacks without technical skills

c. Internet of Things (IoT) Vulnerabilities
Smart devices (cameras, home systems) are poorly secured
Can be used in botnets or spying

d. Supply Chain Attacks
Attackers target trusted software providers
Example: Malware hidden in software updates

e. Deepfake and Social Engineering Attacks
Fake videos or voices used for fraud
Increased identity theft risks
